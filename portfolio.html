Internship

Table Detection in Financial Documents

Location: University of La Rochelle - L3i Laboratory

Period: 2025

Summary: During this internship, I worked on the problem of detecting tables in complex financial documents, which often contain noisy layouts, scanned formats, and irregular structures.

Tasks:

  • Studied and compared three state-of-the-art models: TATR (Transformer-based), TDeLTA (BiLSTM + Attention), and LLaMA3 (prompt-based classification).
  • Built and trained TDeLTA model using PaddleOCR preprocessing, BiLSTM and MultiHeadAttention layers.
  • Evaluated performance on benchmark datasets (PubTabNet, FinTabNet, ICDAR 2013) using metrics like IoU and mean Average Precision (mAP).
  • Implemented postprocessing strategies to eliminate false positives and improve bounding box coherence.

Results: Achieved 92.15% mAP on PubTabNet, demonstrating robustness and accuracy in detecting table boundaries across various layouts.

Challenges: Dealing with noisy OCR output, dataset inconsistencies (e.g. FinTabNet missing annotations), and tuning models with limited resources.

Skills Used: Python, TensorFlow, Keras, CUDA, PaddleOCR, HuggingFace Transformers

Reflection: This internship greatly improved my understanding of sequence modeling and document AI. I learned to adapt between different modeling paradigms and evaluate trade-offs between performance and practicality.
